Kabsa

A traditional Saudi rice dish, often served with chicken or lamb.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 1 hour

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of basmati rice
  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1/4 cup toasted almonds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear, then soak it in water for 20-30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic and chopped tomatoes, and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste, ground cumin, coriander, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, black pepper, and salt. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes.
  7. Add the drained rice to the pot, stir gently, and bring back to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. Fluff the rice with a fork, add raisins and toasted almonds if desired, and serve hot.

Pizza

A classic Italian dish with a variety of toppings and a crispy crust.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 20 minutes

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Ingredients:

  • 1 pizza dough
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup pepperoni slices
  • 1/4 cup sliced olives
  • 1/4 cup sliced bell peppers
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a lightly floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Transfer the dough to a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  4. Spread the pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Sprinkle the mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
  6. Top with pepperoni slices, olives, and bell peppers.
  7. Sprinkle dried oregano over the top.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  9. Remove from the oven, slice, and serve hot.

Cake

A soft and fluffy cake, perfect for any occasion.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 35 minutes

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  5. Mix in the vanilla extract.
  6. Alternately add the flour mixture and milk to the butter mixture, beginning and ending with the flour mixture. Mix until just combined.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Yalanji

A Middle Eastern dish made of grape leaves stuffed with a flavorful rice mixture.

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 1 hour

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar grape leaves, drained and rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups short-grain rice
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rice, onion, parsley, mint, tomatoes, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine.
  2. Lay a grape leaf flat on a clean surface, shiny side down. Place a small spoonful of the rice mixture in the center of the leaf.
  3. Fold the sides of the leaf over the filling and roll tightly from the bottom to the top, creating a small, cigar-shaped roll.
  4. Repeat with the remaining grape leaves and filling.
  5. Place the stuffed grape leaves in a large pot, seam side down, in a single layer. If necessary, make a second layer on top.
  6. Add enough water to the pot to cover the grape leaves by about an inch.
  7. Place a plate on top of the grape leaves to keep them submerged during cooking.
  8. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, until the rice is cooked and the grape leaves are tender.
  9. Remove from the heat and let cool slightly before serving. Yalanji can be served warm or at room temperature.
